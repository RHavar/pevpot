Frequently Asked Questions

What is my chance of winning?

Your chance of winning is how much you sent divided by the amount (of non-dust) sent to the draw. So lets say you send 0.01 BTC, and the total sent during the draw is 200 BTC. You would have a 0.01 / 200 (0.005%) chance of winning 200 BTC (plus sponsorship contributions).

How often does the lottery get drawn?

The lotto is drawn every 1000 bitcoin blocks, which is approximately every week. When the block height ends in 000, that is the draw block and that block and the preceeding 999 blocks are used.

What is the most I can bet?

There is no upper-limit in how much you can bet.

What is considered dust?

Any output less than 0.0001 BTC (100 bits) is considered dust, and excluded

How long until I get my prize?

Between 24 and 48 hours. The first 24 hours give everyone a chance to verify the fairness of the draw, and if there are no disputes the result is finalized and payment will be made. Payments are made manually from cold-storage, which is why up to an additional 24 hours is required.

Can I get a refund?

No, sorry. There are no refunds for players or sponsors possible.

How many confirmations are needed?

As long as it is confirmed in the draw, it counts. If it doesn't get confirmed in time, it will be in the subsequent draw. The draw actually ends in the xxx000 block, but we use the hash from 6 blocks later: xxxxx006 so in many ways, they're actually getting 6 confirmations.

What happens in a block reorg?

Occasionally in bitcoin, the blockchain will go through a reorganization and the hash of a certain block height will change. If this happens to a draw hash, we use the new block hash as it's the only way we can prove it's fair.

What about transaction malleability

We only care about the final transactions in the draw blocks. If transaction ids change, or some transactions were double spent it does not affect the draw.

What prevents a miner discarding blocks?

Once we get a hash, we apply an extremely time-consuming key streching function to it (as outlined in the provably fair), which should take at least half an hour for a very high end computer to compute. This means if a miner wanted to selectively discard blocks, it would be forced to first run this function before publishing the block while hoping to have it orphaned. The economics of this quickly makes it impossible, even for huge rewards.

Why are transactions sorted by txid and not block order

Sorting by block order is a bit conceptually nicer, but significantly harder to get from common APIs. Listing transactions is supported by dozens of services however, so this just makes 3rd party verification much simpler.

Do you support transactions with multiple outputs?

Yes, and they can have multiple outputs to the lottery address themselves. Just remember, all dust outputs are excluded.

Why do you require the winner to sign a message?

There's quite a few reasons for this:

  • It allows people to have the prize to go to cold addresses, or multisig ones
  • It avoids us sending money to unclaimable places
  • Gives us the chance to sort out any mess (e.g. people sending from shared wallets)
